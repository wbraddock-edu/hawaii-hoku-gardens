Landscape Design Trends in Hawaii 2024

Home › Blog
By Haunani PerezMay 22, 2025

Hawaii Landscaping Is Evolving

Hawaii's landscaping and garden design scene is experiencing a renaissance. Driven by ecological awareness, insurance pressures (after the Lahaina fires), and a growing appreciation for native beauty, Hawaii homeowners and designers are moving away from the conventional "green lawn with tropical accidentals" look toward something more intentional and ecologically coherent.

Trend 1: Native Plant Gardens

The biggest trend we're seeing across all islands is a genuine shift toward native Hawaiian plants. Homeowners who previously would have defaulted to imported ornamentals are now specifically requesting native ʻōhiʻa, lobelias, palapalai ferns, and naupaka. This is being driven by a combination of ecological consciousness, native bird-watching culture, and recognition that native plants are simply lower-maintenance once established.

Trend 2: Fire-Smart Landscaping

Post-Lahaina, fire-smart landscaping is no longer a niche concern. Even on rainy Kauai, homeowners with properties in drier areas (South Shore, West Side) are asking for defensible space planning: wider clearances around structures, fire-resistant plantings, and removal of high-fuel vegetation. We expect this trend to accelerate as insurance companies increasingly factor landscaping into risk assessments.

Trend 3: Indoor-Outdoor Living Integration

As homes increasingly blur the line between interior and exterior, landscaping is being designed in concert with architecture. Landscape architects are working from the earliest stages of home design to create continuity between indoor living spaces and outdoor gardens. Covered lanais become natural extensions of interior rooms with carefully curated planting that frames the transition.

Trend 4: Water-Smart Design

Drip irrigation, smart controllers, and rainwater harvesting are becoming standard rather than optional. Across the islands, water costs are rising and climate patterns are becoming less predictable. Clients are investing upfront in water-smart infrastructure that pays for itself within a few years.

Trend 5: Biophilic Design

The research on biophilic design — the idea that humans need daily contact with nature for psychological health — is reaching mainstream Hawaii clients. We're seeing more requests for private garden sanctuaries, meditation gardens, and curated plant collections specifically designed to engage all senses: scent, texture, sound (rustling grasses, water features), and movement.
