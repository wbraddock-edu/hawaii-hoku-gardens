Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about landscaping on Kauai.

Home › FAQ
What areas of Kauai do you serve?
We serve all of Kauai including Kapaa, Lihue, Wailua, Anahola, Princeville, Hanalei, Kilauea, Koloa, Poipu, and Waimea. For remote properties on dirt roads, please call ahead for scheduling.
Do you offer free estimates?
Yes, all estimates are completely free. We always do an on-site visit before quoting — every property is different, and we won't give you a number without seeing your land first. Estimates are usually scheduled within 3–5 days.
Do you use pesticides?
We minimize pesticide use and always default to organic and integrated pest management approaches first. When chemical treatments are necessary, we use targeted applications and always inform clients about what was used and when it's safe to re-enter the area. We never spray near reef-adjacent waterways.
Can you work around my vacation rental bookings?
Absolutely. Many of our clients have vacation rentals, and we're experienced at scheduling around guest stays. We work with your booking calendar and can adjust scheduled visits when guests are arriving. We're quiet and professional when guests are present.
What's the best time of year for landscaping projects on Kauai?
Kauai's climate is suitable for landscaping year-round. The drier months of May–September are ideal for large installations and hardscaping projects. The wetter months of November–March are excellent for planting, as rains help establish new plants. We work year-round and plan projects around both your schedule and seasonal conditions.
Do you specialize in native Hawaiian plants?
Yes, this is one of our specialties. Haunani has formal training in native Hawaiian plant ecosystems. We source native plants from reputable nurseries on Kauai and the Big Island, and we can create fully native gardens that support local birds, insects, and ecological networks. Native plants, once established, also require significantly less water and maintenance.
What's included in a garden design consultation?
Our design consultation ($350) includes: a 1.5-hour on-site visit with Haunani, assessment of soil, drainage, and sun exposure, a detailed design proposal with plant recommendations, a rough budget estimate, and one revision. If you hire us for installation, we credit the consultation fee toward the project.
Are you licensed and insured?
Yes. Hoku Gardens holds a Hawaii C-27 Landscaping Contractor license and carries full general liability insurance and workers compensation coverage. We're fully insured on all projects, protecting both our team and your property.
